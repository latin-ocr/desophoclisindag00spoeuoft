CAPUT II.

De tempore, quo scripta sit fabula.

In prolegomenis, quae Huntius primae Indagatorum edi-
tioni praemisit, legimus pagT 34: „But in any case our play
may reasonably be placed considerably earlier than this (sc.
Aristoph. Ranae), if only on account of its metrical strictne88.''
et eodem fere tempore Wilamowitzius scribit 1): „je langer ich
mich mit den Ichneuten beschffltige, um so fester wird mein
Glaube, dasz sie ein Jugendwerk sind.''; eodem denique redeunt,
quae apud ultimum Indagatorum editorem 2) annotata in-
venimus: „General impressione are less to be trusted, but I
think that few will hesitate on consideration to acquiesce in
the judgment of Wilamowitz, that the play reflects Sophocles’
earlier manner ........ But Wilamowitz finds unmistakable
signs of an early date in certain definite peculiarities of
technique; . . . .⁷'

Eiusmodi virorum doctorum rarissimum consensum (nam
complures etiam alii nominari possunt), nisi firmis exortum
argumentis, fieri non potuisse constat ; nihilominus haud inutile
mihi visum est, quae alii alias attulere, in unum conferre
atque distinguere, quae minoris momenti quaeve maioris, quae
denique nulli obnoxia dubitationi censeam.
